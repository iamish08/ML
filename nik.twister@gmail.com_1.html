observation

There are 306 datapoints in the dataset.
There are no null values for any column.

we have 3 features as follows :-  
age : the age of patient at time of operation  
year : the year in which operation took place (1964 will be written as 64)  
nodes : the number of positive axillary nodes.  

The target column is 'status':-  
status : 1 -> patient survived >=5 years  
         2 -> patient died within 5 years  

The no. of datapoints for which status is 1 -> 225
The no. of datapoints for which status is 2 -> 81

#barplot where x = year and y = % of people who later survived after undergoing operation in that year. 
x = [58,59,60,61,62,63,64,65,66,67,68,69]
y = []
dfs = haberman.groupby('year')
for tup in dfs:
    df = tup[1]          
    # (people who later survived)/(total people who underwent operation in that year)
    y.append(len(df[df['status']==1])/len(df))

sns.barplot(x=x,y=y)
Out[215]:
<matplotlib.axes._subplots.AxesSubplot at 0x7fd2d1ef7c88>

obervations

1) From the barplot it is clearly evident that 1965 is the year in which out of all the people who
   underwent operation that year , % of people who later survived is lowest as compared to all other years. 
   On the other hand 1961 had the best % of survivors.
2) Looking at the boxplot and distribution plot for 'year', it can be inferred that this feature is unfit
   for classification b/w classes on its own.
